The Problem

In emergencies, vulnerable individuals—especially women, children, and the elderly—often can't access a phone or speak for help. Existing tools are visible, costly, or unusable under stress.

The Solution

VibePatch is a discreet, skin-worn safety patch activated by a simple tap pattern. It silently sends an SOS alert and GPS location to emergency contacts without needing a phone, voice, or visible action.

What It Does

When tapped in a preset pattern, VibePatch vibrates for confirmation and transmits a silent SOS alert using Bluetooth, GSM, or Wi-Fi, optionally sharing GPS data if connected to a mobile device.

How It Works

VibePatch uses a tap sensor, vibration motor, microcontroller (Arduino or ESP32), and wireless module. The system detects a specific gesture and triggers an alert, sent quietly to designated contacts in seconds.

Design Process

The concept began from observing rising safety concerns in India. Early research focused on replacing voice with gestures for emergency alerts. Prototyping will begin using affordable hardware to simulate the alert system and refine comfort and reliability.

Why It's Unique

Unlike phones or apps, VibePatch works silently, without screens or speech. It looks like a regular bandage but can trigger life-saving alerts. It's affordable, scalable, and ideal for schools, public transport, and eldercare.
